Dombolo

Steamed bread

Steam Bread, or dombolo, is a beloved staple in many African countries, particularly in Southern Africa. Unlike traditional baked bread, steam bread is cooked by steaming rather than baking, which gives it a distinctively soft, moist, and fluffy texture.

Ingidients

  • 480 g flour
  • 2 t yeast
  • 2 T sugar
  • 1 T salt
  • 2-2 1/2 cups water

Steps:

  1. Mix the Dough: Combine flour, yeast, sugar, and salt, then add warm water (or milk) and mix until it forms a soft dough.
  2. Knead and Rise: Knead the dough until smooth, then cover it and let it rise in a warm place until it doubles in size, which usually takes about 1-2 hours.
  3. Prepare for Steaming: Grease a heat-safe container, like a metal or enamel bowl, and place the risen dough inside. Some people also wrap the dough in a cloth or plastic wrap to help maintain the shape and prevent moisture from entering.
  4. Steam the Bread: Place the container inside a larger pot with a few inches of boiling water, making sure the water doesnâ€™t reach the bread. Cover tightly and steam for about an hour to an hour and a half. The steam cooks the dough gently, resulting in a soft, spongy bread.
  5. Cool and Serve: Once done, carefully remove the container and allow the bread to cool slightly before slicing.
